IMPERATIVE VERBS

Imperative verbs are written in the base form.They do indicate number or tense.

Look at the pictures. Read the instructions on how to make a cup of coffee.

  • Boil some water.
  • Put a teaspoon of instant coffee powder into a cup.
  • Pour some boiling water into the cup.
  • Add some sugar to the coffee.
  • Stir the coffee for a few seconds and it is ready.
The words in bold are imperative verbs.

We use imperative verbs when we tell someone to do something or when we give instructions.

More examples:

  1. Come here.
  2. Stand up.
  3. Study hard.
  4. Sit down.
  5. Go this way.
  6. Open the door for me.
  7. Greet your teachers when you meet them.
  8. Post this letter for me.
  9. Send the naughty boy to the principal.
  10. Take proper care of your pets.
  11. Go to bed early.
  12. Wash your hands before and after every meal.
  13. Brush your teeth before going to bed.
  14. Help your mother when she is busy.
  15. Ask the teacher for permission to leave the classroom.
  16. Drink more water to keep healthy.
  17. Speak English with your friends.
  18. Send the sick boy to the hospital.
  19. Wear a jacket when you feel cold.
  20. Try to be happy.
