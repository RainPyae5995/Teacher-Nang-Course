POSSESSIVE PRONOUNS

Possessive pronouns are used to show ownership or possession.

Look at the pictures.Read the sentences.

1. The boy has a pencil.
    The pencil is his.

2. The girl has a book.
    The book is hers.

3. The storybook is mine.
    I bought it yesterday.

4. This wallet belongs to you.
    It is yours.

5. The men have a car.
    The car is theirs.

6. This parrot belongs to David and me.
    It is ours.

The words `his`, `hers`, `mine`, `theirs` and `ours` are pronouns.
Pronouns which show possession are called possessive pronouns.

Possessive pronouns are used to show that something belongs to somebody.

More examples:

  1. This new bicycle belongs to my cousin, Adrian. It is his.
  2. The farmers have sold their sheep. The sheep are no more theirs.
  3. We have our dinner at six o'clock while they have theirs at seven.
  4. I wish to borrow your bicycle, not hers.
  5. Here is your hat, but where is mine?
  6. This school is big, but ours is bigger.
  7. Please return that pen to me. It is mine.
  8. Don't take away the book. It is not yours.
  9. Those are her pencils. They are hers.
  10. This is your dictionary. It is yours.
  11. This is my sharpener. This sharpener is mine.
  12. These files belong to you. They are yours.
  13. This is your eraser. This eraser is yours.
  14. These are our rulers. The rulers are ours.
  15. Those school bags belong to us. They are ours.
  16. Please pass me that bag. It is mine.
  17. Don't take away the water bottle. It is not yours.
  18. Here are two pens. Which one is hers and which is his?
  19. There is still much to do, so we can only do ours, not theirs.
  20. Those are her clothes. They are hers, not mine.
