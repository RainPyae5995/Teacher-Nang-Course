Can we count `chicks`, `boys` and `watches`? Yes,we can.

Can we count `jam`, `milk` and `water`? No,we can't.

Countable nouns are nouns that can be counted.

Certains quantifiers are used with countable nouns. They are `some`, `many`, `a few`, `a lot of `, `a plenty of ` and `several`.

Singular countable nouns cannot be used alone. We have to put `a` or `an` before the noun.

We put `a` if the noun begins with consonant sound and `an` if it begins with a vowel sound.

Examples:

  • a handphone
  • an aeroplane
  • a few coins
  • several kittens
  • some singers
  • many birds
  • a lot of ants
  • plenty of flowers

We cannot count things like `water`, `sand`, `hair`, `salt` and `sugar`.These are uncountable nouns.

Uncountable nouns are nouns that cannot be counted.

Certains quantifiers are used with uncountable nouns. They are `some`, `much`, `a little`, `plenty of ` and `a lot of `.

We can also use words like `a glass of `, `a bowl of `, `a bucket of `, etc.with uncountable nouns.

Examples:

  • some ice
  • much water
  • a little jam
  • plenty of food
  • a lot of rubbish
  • a glass of milk
  • a bowl of rice
  • a bucket of sand
