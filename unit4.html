COLLECTIVE NOUNS AND ABSTRACT NOUNS

  • COLLECTIVE NOUNS
  • Look at the pictures.Read the words.

a litter of kittens

a flock of sheep

a bunch of keys

a team of players

The words `litter` and `flock` show a number of animals grouped together.

The words `bunch` shows a number of things grouped together.

The words `team` shows a number of people grouped together.

`Litter`, `flock`, `bunch`, `team` are called collective nouns.

Collective nouns refer to groups of people, animals or things.

More examples:
  1. Our class has thirty-five students.
  2. Is the Chinese badminton team the strongest in the world?
  3. A group of children is playing games.
  4. A pack of wolves lives in the forest.
  5. The basket of fruits is in the kitchen.
  6. A troop of monkeys is climbing up the tree.
  7. The hitch hikers were attacked by a swarm of bees.
  8. The police have arrested the whole gang of kidnappers.
  9. The bunch of keys belongs to me.
  10. The farmer has just sold his herd of cattle.
  • ABSTRACT NOUNS
  • Look at the pictures.Read the sentences.

The principal explained the importance of punctuality.

The pupils respect their teacher for her patience and dedication.

A large number of nouns refer to qualities, ideas and conditions. Examples are `punctuality`, `patience`, `dedication`, `love`, `honesty`, `importance` and `sickness`. Such nouns are abstract in nature and are therefore called abstract nouns.

Abstract nouns are words that represent a quality, an idea or a condition.

  1. The workers were praised for their loyalty and diligence.
  2. The teacher has many years of teaching experience.
  3. Procrastination is a thief of time.
  4. To do him justice,he is a kind-hearted man.
  5. The teacher explained the importance of sincerity.
